## Summary
This course served primarily as a structured review and formalization of concepts I had previously encountered through enterprise IT, software development, and self-study. Rather than introducing entirely new ideas, it strengthened my understanding of the networking, security, infrastructure, and operational principles that underpin cloud architecture.

The most valuable outcome was developing a more grounded systems-level understanding of how networking layers, secure communication, DNS trust, redundancy, and service responsibility boundaries work together in distributed systems and cloud environments.

## Problem & Constraints
My background included practical exposure to:

- enterprise networking and endpoint support,
- Active Directory and MFA workflows,
- VPNs and least-privilege access,
- virtualization and backups,
- Linux and Dockerized environments,
- and full-stack software development.

However, much of that experience was operational or implementation-focused rather than explicitly architectural.

This course helped connect those practical experiences into clearer mental models around:

- OSI/networking flow and encapsulation,
- routing and DNS trust relationships,
- public/private key cryptography,
- hashing and digital signatures,
- stateful vs stateless security models,
- redundancy and disaster recovery concepts,
- and cloud infrastructure/service models.

## Lessons & Next Moves
The strongest takeaway was realizing how much cloud architecture depends on foundational networking, security, and operational understanding rather than isolated AWS service knowledge.

The course also reinforced that my current SquadSync/Soccer-Subber work is largely cloud-agnostic at the application level, while still naturally mapping toward future AWS integration through managed infrastructure, monitoring, CI/CD, event-driven workflows, and serverless compute patterns.

This section established the foundation for continuing deeper AWS-focused study through the Solutions Architect Associate path and future cloud-integrated PoC work.
